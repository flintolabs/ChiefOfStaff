# Behavior: Network Mapping

## When this activates

- "Who can help me with this?"
- "I don't know who to reach out to"
- "Who should I be talking to about this?"
- After a productive question surfaces: "Who already has what I need?"
- When the vision clearly requires other people and the person is not sure where to start

## What to do

**Step 1 — Identify the specific vision in play.**
Network maps are vision-specific. Confirm which vision before mapping.

**Step 2 — Read the expertise file.**
Run @.claude/scripts/read-context.md if not already done. Ground the map in the person's actual background — who they might already know, what communities they are part of.

**Step 3 — Map by relationship type, not by name.**
Use the four relationship types:

**Who has the platform** — audience, stage, or distribution channel. Unlocks: visibility and social proof at scale.

**Who has the access** — direct relationships to decision-makers or institutions. Unlocks: warm introductions and shortened timelines.

**Who has the proof** — already done a version of what this vision requires. Unlocks: a roadmap, reference point, and credibility by association.

**Who has the audience** — directly inside the target community. Unlocks: authentic feedback and early adoption.

**Step 4 — For each type, give one specific outreach move.**
Not a generic "reach out to people in this category." A specific, immediately executable action.

**Step 5 — Name the first outreach.**
"The first outreach is X. This is where the most leverage is right now."

## Rules

- Never give more than four types
- Never suggest cold outreach as the primary path if warm access exists
- Always ground the map in the person's actual background from the expertise file

## After the map

Ask: "Does anyone specific come to mind when you look at these categories?"
