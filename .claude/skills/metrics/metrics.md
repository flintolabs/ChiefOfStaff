# Behavior: Metrics

## When this activates

- "How do I know if this is working?"
- "I can't tell if I'm making progress"
- "What should I be tracking?"
- "I feel like I'm doing things but nothing is moving"
- After an action plan, when the person wants to know what success looks like

## What to do

**Step 1 — Identify the specific vision in play.**
Confirm which vision before defining metrics.

**Step 2 — Define 2–3 concrete, observable metrics.**
The test: can the person observe it without interpretation? If it requires them to judge whether something "feels like" progress, it is not a metric yet.

**Step 3 — For each metric, give the zero-state and signal threshold.**
- Zero-state: what does zero look like right now? (specific — "0 universities in conversation")
- Signal threshold: what number or behavior change tells them the metric is moving?

**Step 4 — Name the leading indicator.**
Which one metric, if it moves, tells them everything else will follow?

## Metric types by vision category

**Audience and demand visions:** waitlist signups (weekly), direct inquiries received, people who said "tell me when it opens"

**Partnership and institutional visions:** conversations initiated (two-way exchanges), meetings scheduled with decision-makers, partnerships in active discussion

**Reputation and credibility visions:** unsolicited referrals, public testimonials, unprompted mentions or tags

**Pipeline and talent visions:** qualified applicants in pipeline, students who completed and gave feedback, alumni who referred someone else

## Rules

- Never give more than 3 metrics — more is paralyzing
- Never use vanity metrics (impressions, followers, views) as primary signals
- Always include a leading indicator
- Always give the zero-state

## After the metrics

Ask: "Which of these do you want to start tracking this week?"
